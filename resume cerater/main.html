Types of Resumes
Chronological Resume
A good choice if your work history shows a consistent career path, like working in the same industry for several years with each role being more senior than the last.
Functional Resume
A good choice if you have gaps in your work history, as it focuses on skills and experience over work history.
Combination Resume
Also called a hybrid resume, this format combines a reverse-chronological work history with your functional skills and relevant experiences.
Non-traditional Resume
A non-traditional resume is a creative way to present your skills, education, and experience to potential employers. It can include unique features such as videos, graphics, images, infographics, portfolios, or custom web pages. Non-traditional resumes can be digital or physical, and they can be a good way to stand out from other candidates.
Resume Profile
A resume profile is a short introductory paragraph that highlights your skills, experience, and achievements, and how they relate to a specific job opening. It's a professional introduction to your resume that's similar to a short cover letter.
Infographic Resume
A resume that uses different colors, typefaces, and pictures to display your design skills. You can hand it out during interviews, post it on social media, or attach it to email applications. Nontraditional resume
